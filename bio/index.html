Professional Bio


Scene

I was born and raised in Milwaukee, WI. I always possessed the desire to attend college but had very little idea of what I wanted to become. I was offered scholarships to a few universities to play soccer, including Rockford University and Xavier in Chicago, IL. After a lot of thought, my heart kept me in Milwaukee near to my family. Pursuit of education ensued at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee (UWM).

Undergrad

Prior to declaring psychology as my undergraduate major of study, my chosen coursework focused on topics in philosophy, language, anthropology, mythology, and other general studies. With a family history of attorneys on my father’s side, I had placed myself in-line to attend law school after undergrad. For much of my undergrad, I worked in a local law firm in downtown Milwaukee, gaining professional and legal experience. Then came Psychology; my experience in UWM’s undergraduate program through the department of psychology centered my interests in topics of cognition and neuroscience. Not to make things more difficult, admission into Dr. Deborah Hannula’s MINDfull Memory research lab, and collaboration with the very wonderful graduate and post-doc members, routed these interests into a sharpened scope of practice.

UNMC

After I completed my bachelors in psychology at UWM, I decided to take some time away from school to advance my professional experiences. After a bit of confusion, followed by an enlightening personal life decision, on July 11th of 2016 I assumed the position of Clinical Research Associate in the Warren Neuroscience Lab – Department of Neurological Sciences at the University of Nebraska Medical Center (UNMC) alongside Dr. David Warren and the exceptional faculty.

UNMC

I am so excited to continue under the mentorship of Dr. David Warren as I have already learned so much in just the first few months here at UNMC. I will be strengthening my skills with eye-tracking methods and learning: administration of neuropsychological tests, use of various neuroimaging tools and techniques, administration of neurostimulation – WOW!, how to work with populations with neurodevelopmental disorders and neurodegenerative diseases, and I am sure, many other skills not reported here.
